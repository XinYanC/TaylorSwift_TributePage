About Taylor Swift:

  • December 13, 1989 Swift was born in Reading, Pennsylvania.
  • October 24, 2006 Swift releases her self-titled debut album, a collection of country-pop tunes the singer wrote during her freshman year of high school. Led by the Top 20 single "Tim McGraw," it peaks at #5 on the Albums chart and becomes the longest-charting album of the 2000s in the US, logging 157 weeks by 2009.
  • November 11, 2008 Eighteen-year-old Swift releases her second album, Fearless, which goes on to sell over 10 million copies in America. Standout tracks include "Love Story" and "You Belong With Me."
  • September 13, 2009 When Taylor Swift wins for Best Female Video at the MTV Video Music Awards, Kanye West comes on stage and commandeers the microphone, explaining that Beyonc√© deserved the award for her "Single Ladies" video.
  • October 25, 2010 Swift releases her third album, Speak Now.
  • August 21, 2012 With 623,000 digital copies sold, Taylor Swift's hit "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together" becomes the single with the most digital sales for a female artist.
  • October 22, 2012 Swift releases her fourth album, Red.
  • October 27, 2014 Swift issues 1989, her biggest-selling album. The first two singles are monster hits: "Shake It Off" and "Blank Space."
  • June 21, 2015 As Apple readies to launch its new streaming platform, Apple Music, Taylor Swift writes an open letter to the company threatening to withhold her album 1989 if artists aren't paid their deserved royalties.
  • November 10, 2017 Swift releases her sixth album, Reputation.
  • August 23, 2019 Swift releases Lover, an upbeat album that stands in contrast to her previous release, the serpentine Reputation.
  • July 24, 2020 Swift releases her eighth album, Folklore.
  • November 22, 2020 For the third consecutive year, Taylor Swift wins Artist of the Year at the American Music Awards.
